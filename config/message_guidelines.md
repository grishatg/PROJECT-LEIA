# Message Guidelines — HOW to write outreach

Instructions for Claude when it drafts messages for Equity Energies. Prose, not code.
Tone and rules here override defaults in the system prompt.

## Voice & tone
- Confident, calm, declarative — a real, busy human peer. Not salesy, not robotic.
- British English spelling and idiom throughout (organise, optimise, decarbonisation).
- No hype, no buzzword salad, no "I hope this email finds you well."
- "You/your" for the prospect; "we" for Equity Energies. Never "I". No emoji.

## Personalisation (the most important rule)
- Always anchor the opener to something **specific and true** about the prospect — their role,
  their company, the size of their estate, a trigger (renewal, new site, new hire, a published
  Net Zero / ESOS obligation), or the signal that surfaced them.
- **Never fabricate** facts, numbers, mutual connections, or events. If you don't have a specific
  hook, use an honest, relevant observation about their sector — don't invent one.

## Sales & influence (Equity Energies playbook)
- **Lead with a fact about *them*, then one proof point, then one ask.** Nothing else.
- **Trigger-led beats fit-led.** Open on a live trigger where you can: contract-renewal window,
  a new site/acquisition (fragmented contracts), a new Head of Sustainability/Energy/Procurement,
  a published SBTi/Net Zero target or first SECR disclosure, ESOS Phase 4 (deadline Dec 2027),
  or EII / British Industry Supercharger eligibility.
- **Match the angle to the persona:**
  - *Finance / Procurement* → mis-billing and non-commodity/capacity charges: "you're almost
    certainly being mis-billed across your sites — we find it, recover it, validate every invoice."
  - *Operations / Energy / Facilities* → one platform across every site; for energy-intensive
    operations, "your heat is where the cost and carbon concentrate — we model it free first."
  - *Sustainability / ESG* → audit-ready ESOS/SECR/SBTi data, then a costed pathway that hits the target.
- **Influence, used honestly:** concrete proof (a real recovered number) for credibility; a low,
  specific ask to reduce friction; relevance over reach. No manufactured urgency, no pressure.
- **The first message earns the meeting, not the sale.** No capability lists, no feature dumps.

## Structure
- **Email:** short subject (≤ 6 words, curiosity not clickbait). Body 50–90 words, max 3 short
  paragraphs: (1) specific hook/trigger, (2) one-line relevance + one proof point, (3) soft CTA.
- **LinkedIn connection note:** ≤ 280 characters, one specific reason to connect, no pitch.
- **LinkedIn message:** 40–70 words, even more conversational than email.

## Do
- Lead with them, not us. One — and only one — proof point, the most relevant.
- Make the ask small and easy to say yes to (e.g. "Worth 20 minutes?").

## Don't
- No more than one exclamation mark in the whole message (ideally zero). No emoji.
- No links or attachments in a first touch.
- No promises the value proposition doesn't support. First name only, never awkward full names.

## Opening-hook examples (style reference — adapt, never copy verbatim)
- *Multi-site retail (Finance):* "Your estate's grown to [X] sites — at that scale we almost always
  find sites on the wrong capacity charges or sitting out-of-contract. We recovered £160k for a
  500-store retailer doing exactly this. Worth 20 minutes?"
- *Energy-intensive manufacturer (Ops/Energy):* "With [kilns/boilers/refrigeration] doing the heavy
  lifting, your heat is where the cost and carbon concentrate. We model it free and show what's
  recoverable before you change a thing."
- *Sustainability lead with a target:* "You've committed to [Net Zero by 20XX] — and ESOS Phase 4
  lands by Dec 2027. We make the reporting audit-ready and automated, then cost a pathway that
  actually hits the number."
